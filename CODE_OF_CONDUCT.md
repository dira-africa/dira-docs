# Code of Conduct — Dira Africa

> *"Your data earns your airtime. Your data grows your crops. Your data builds your safety net."*

## Our Commitment

Dira Africa is building a climate data utility for smallholder farmers across Sub-Saharan Africa. The communities we serve are among the most vulnerable on Earth: farmers whose livelihoods depend on accurate weather data, rural youth earning income as Data Agents, and families in food-insecure counties who need the safety net we are building.

The open-source community that builds Dira must reflect the values of dignity, inclusion, and mutual respect that we ask of the communities we serve. We are committed to making participation in the Dira project a harassment-free, welcoming, and intellectually honest experience for everyone — regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

### Behaviour we actively encourage

**Be kind and assume good faith.** Most contributors are volunteers giving their time to a mission they believe in. Start from the assumption that people mean well.

**Be specific and constructive in criticism.** "This function will cause a race condition under concurrent load — here is how to fix it" is useful. "This is terrible code" is not. Focus on the work, not the person.

**Acknowledge the real-world stakes.** Changes to Dira's payment flows, GPS data handling, or AI verification pipeline affect real people's incomes and food security. Acknowledge this weight, even in technical discussions.

**Welcome people at all experience levels.** Dira explicitly aims to be accessible to non-technical founders and contributors. Condescension toward people with less technical experience is not acceptable. If someone asks a question you consider basic, answer it with the same care you would want if you were learning.

**Credit people properly.** If you build on someone's idea, implementation, or research — acknowledge it. This includes crediting field knowledge contributors (Swahili translators, agricultural domain experts) with the same respect as engineers.

**Respect language diversity.** Dira operates in English and Swahili. Welcome contributions in either language. Do not signal that Swahili contributions are less valuable than English ones.

**Listen to community feedback.** The farmers and Data Agents who use the platform are not just end users — they are the reason the project exists. When field operations staff or community members report that something is confusing, broken, or harmful, take that seriously.

**Be honest about uncertainty.** If you are not sure whether your code is correct, say so. If a security concern is unresolved, flag it. Intellectual honesty is more valuable than appearing confident.

### Behaviour that is not acceptable

**Harassment in any form.** This includes:
- Offensive comments related to gender, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, disability, mental illness, neuroatypicality, physical appearance, body size, age, race, national origin, ethnic origin, nationality, immigration status, language, religion or lack thereof, or any other identity attribute
- Deliberate misgendering, use of rejected names, or repeated deadnaming
- Physical threats, threats of violence, or incitement of violence toward any individual or group
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances of any kind, including sexualised comments, imagery, or simulated sexual contact in any community space

**Intimidation and silencing.** This includes:
- Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following (online or in person)
- Sustained disruption of discussions, presentations, or community events
- Dogpiling — multiple community members targeting a single person with criticism, especially in public channels
- Threatening to publish or publishing someone's private information without their explicit consent ("doxxing")

**Dishonesty and bad faith.** This includes:
- Deliberately misrepresenting another person's words, code, or intentions
- Taking credit for work that is not yours
- Knowingly introducing security vulnerabilities or harmful code
- Coordinated inauthentic behaviour — for example, using multiple accounts to inflate apparent community support for a position

**Exploitation of vulnerable communities.** Dira serves low-income, low-literacy users who are trusting the platform with their personal data and financial transactions. Any behaviour — inside or outside code review — that would undermine that trust, exploit that vulnerability, or cause harm to the farmers and agents who depend on the platform is a serious violation of this Code of Conduct.

**Abuse of community spaces.** This includes:
- Spam, off-topic promotional content, or repeated unsolicited outreach
- Using community channels to recruit Dira contributors for unrelated commercial ventures
- Disruptive behaviour in GitHub Issues, Discussions, or pull request reviews that prevents the community from doing productive work

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all Dira community spaces, including:

- All four GitHub repositories (`dira-core`, `dira-api`, `dira-docs`, `dira-contracts`)
- GitHub Issues, Pull Requests, Discussions, and code review comments
- The Dira community Telegram groups (Data Agents, farmers, and internal)
- Email communications at any `@dira.africa` address
- Any event (online or in-person) where you are representing the Dira project
- Social media interactions where you are publicly acting as a Dira contributor

It also applies to behaviour **outside** these spaces when that behaviour directly affects the safety or wellbeing of community members or the communities we serve. For example: harassment of a Dira contributor on a personal social media account, or behaviour toward field operations partners that undermines trust in the platform.

## Reporting

### How to report a violation

If you experience or witness behaviour that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it. You can do so:

**By email:** conduct@dira.africa
This inbox is monitored by two senior members of the Dira Africa team. Your report will be treated with confidentiality.

**By direct message to a maintainer:** If you are not comfortable emailing, you may contact any listed maintainer directly via GitHub.

**By anonymous report:** If you prefer to report anonymously, use the anonymous form linked from the project README. Anonymous reports may limit our ability to follow up but will always be investigated.

### What to include in your report

- Your contact information (if you are comfortable providing it)
- The names or usernames of the people involved
- A description of the behaviour, including direct quotes or screenshots where available
- The location and time of the incident (which platform, which thread, which event)
- Any context you think is relevant
- Whether you believe there is an ongoing safety risk

You do not need to have a perfect or complete account to make a report. We would rather investigate an uncertain report than have someone suffer in silence.

### What happens after you report

1. **Acknowledgement** — You will receive confirmation that your report has been received within **24 hours**.
2. **Initial review** — The conduct team will review the report and, if necessary, take immediate interim measures to protect the safety of involved parties.
3. **Investigation** — We will gather information from all relevant parties. We will not share your identity as a reporter without your consent unless required to do so by law.
4. **Decision** — The conduct team will reach a decision within **7 days** for most cases. Complex cases may take longer; we will keep you informed.
5. **Communication** — You will be informed of the outcome, within the limits of what we are able to share while respecting the privacy of all parties.
6. **Appeal** — Any party may appeal a conduct decision by contacting conduct@dira.africa with new information or a substantive argument that the process was not followed correctly.

## Enforcement

The Dira conduct team has the following enforcement tools, applied in proportion to the severity and context of the violation. Repeated violations escalate through these levels.

### Level 1 — Correction

**Used for:** A single instance of mild unprofessional language, an unintentionally dismissive comment, or an off-topic post.

**Action:** A private, written message from the conduct team explaining what was problematic, why it is inconsistent with our standards, and what appropriate behaviour looks like. The conduct team may request a public or private apology.

**Consequence of non-compliance:** Escalation to Level 2.

---

### Level 2 — Warning

**Used for:** A single serious violation, or a pattern of mild violations following a Level 1 correction.

**Action:** A formal written warning with specific instructions about required behaviour changes. The person is placed on notice that a further violation will result in a temporary ban. The warning is logged internally.

**Restriction:** No interaction with the person or community spaces being protected for a specified period (typically 7–30 days). This includes unprompted interactions in public channels, comments on the affected person's PRs, or contact through any Dira-affiliated channel.

**Consequence of non-compliance or further violation:** Escalation to Level 3.

---

### Level 3 — Temporary Ban

**Used for:** A sustained pattern of violations, a serious violation including harassment, or violation of the terms of a Level 2 warning.

**Action:** A temporary ban from all Dira community spaces. Duration is set by the conduct team based on severity (typically 30–180 days). All open PRs from the banned person are paused and will not be merged during the ban period.

**Consequence of violation of ban terms or further violation upon return:** Escalation to Level 4.

---

### Level 4 — Permanent Ban

**Used for:** A pattern of violations that demonstrates an unwillingness to engage with community standards, a single severe violation (including threats of violence, sexual harassment, or deliberate exploitation of the communities we serve), or violation of a temporary ban.

**Action:** Permanent removal from all Dira community spaces with no pathway to reinstatement. All open PRs are closed. The person's contributions that have already been merged are not removed — Apache 2.0 does not permit that — but they will not be invited to participate further.

## Conflict of Interest

Conduct team members who have a personal relationship with either the reporter or the reported person must recuse themselves from handling that case. In such cases, an external member of the open-source community may be invited to participate in the review.

Conduct team members must not discuss ongoing investigations with anyone outside the conduct team, except as required by law.

## A note on power and context

Dira's community spans very different contexts of power and experience. Engineers with decades of experience reviewing the work of first-time contributors; majority shareholders reviewing community members; English-speaking contributors reviewing Swahili translations; staff from high-income countries engaging with communities from low-income ones.

We ask all community members — but especially those with more experience, seniority, or structural power — to be conscious of these dynamics. A dismissive comment from a senior engineer in a code review can silence a first-time contributor permanently. An offhand remark about a language or culture can communicate to an entire community of potential contributors that they are not welcome.

This does not mean senior contributors must suppress honest technical feedback. It means that honest technical feedback and respectful, encouraging engagement are not mutually exclusive — and that it is the responsibility of those with more power to invest in the combination.
